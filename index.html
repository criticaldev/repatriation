FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is repatriation?

Repatriation is the process or act of returning or moving funds from your assets in India to your country of residence or citizenship. It can also been seen as outward remittance from India.

Why repatriate?

This is usually done for different personal reasons such as need for education expenses, medical or healthcare expenses, pay off loans, invest in higher returns, protect from currency depreciation among others.

How much can be repatriated?

Reserve bank of India allows a maximum 1 million US dollars per year to be repatriated.

Why can't I just wire transfer?

Indian banking system will not clear large value wire transfers to destination accounts outside of India unless they originating account is NRE account in India. Upto $10,000 are limit per person in single transaction or carry in person

Why do I have to pay taxes?

Indian regulations were put in place that requires income or capital gains generated from assets in India are paying their fair share of local taxes before they are taken out of the country.
